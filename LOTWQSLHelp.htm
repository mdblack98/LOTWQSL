LOTWQSL 1.11

By W9MDB Michael Black mdblack98@yahoo.com - http://qrz.com/db/W9MDB

Note: If you ever find your LOTW counts dont match the LOTW website numbers download them all again by using 1900-01-01 for the Since date and they should agree after that.

LOTWQSL tracks your QSLs from LOTW for WAS, DXCC, and DXCC Challenge awards. The results will match what you see on the LOTW website so you don't have to go to the website anymore to see your status or keep local records to figure out what you need. LOTWQSL is a much quicker way to see what has occurred in your award status. It can also produce a US map and a grid to show you your status of the states by mode and a list of them and also a bearing to other states if you have a steerable antenna. Note that it does not track QSOs since only QSLs count for awards. It is possible that the # of QSLs shown may be different than what LOTW shows. This was fixed in 1.7.0 but may require you to download your logbook again with 1900-01-01 for the Since date.

LOTWQSL does not have a map to track countries other than giving a count of total countries and letting you know when a new DXCC occurs. A world map of countries does not make for a good visual display due to the variety of country sizes making small ones almost invisible and the ever-changing country borders and names.

Running the first time

When the program is run the first time you need to put in your LOTW login name and password which will be remembered. You can leave the default "Since" date of 1900-01-01 which should cover any ham under the age of 117 J. You should never have to change the date unless you want to re-download from an earlier date. The first download will take the longest time (perhaps quite a few minutes if you have a lot of QSLs). After the download is complete you will see info scrolling by showing new states, countries, etc. and a summary of the numbers at the end of the window if you scroll to the bottom. Note: the "prefixes" is a count of the WPX prefixes you have and not the LOTW count of all the WPX award combinations which is quite complicated. Also note that you need to have a LOTW certificate before you can download your QSLs. If you run TrustedQSL you already have one. Otherwise see this link http://www.arrl.org/instructions

If you want to reload everything at any time just put "1900-01-01" in the Since box and click Refresh. That will reset everything.

If you want to keep track of your QSLs from a certain starting point put an exclamation point in front of the date, e.g. "!2016-01-01". This will only use QSOs from that date forward.

The Refresh now presents a choice of LOTW or importing an ADIF file from your local computer. It turns out that LOTW card checks do not translate over to the LOTW downloaded ADIF file for either WAS or DXCC as of July 20, 2017. So you have to go to the Awards section on LOTW and choose the DXCC account, then download the DXCC QSL Download which will contain your card check QSLs. You can then import that file and the # of countries on LOTW and LOTWQSL will match. We are waiting to hear back from LOTW about the WAS problem and hope that the long term solution is they put both DXCC and WAS card checks into the LOTW account too.

Mismatched mode QSLs

You may see "Mismatched mode" entries during Refresh. As of 2016-12-14 LOTW allows mismatched modes such as JT65=JT9 to count for Digital or TriplePlay awards, but not for band/mode awards. These rows (including the header row) can be copy/pasted into Excel so they're easily examined and tracked if you wish. You may also see people with "DATA" or such as modes that don't match yours. The only way to fix these is either you or your QSO partner must fix their log entry and re-upload the fixed entry to LOTW. These mismatches do count as QSLs but do not count for ANY WAS mode awards since the modes don't match. If you download your ADIF file from LOTW you can find these mismatches with the string "<APP_LoTW_QSLMODE:". If you do get your mismatches fixed you will need to reload your entire log again with "1900-01-01". That will reset the mismatch count.

Multiple call signs

By default you will get QSLs for all of your call signs registered on LOTW. If you want to keep call signs separate use your login "@othercall". For example, W9MDB@W9MDB will use W9MDB only and not any other callsigns. W9MDB@KC9YHH would then get my 2nd call sign separately and keep the QSLs separate from W9MDB. You can switch back and forth between these "@" login names at any time by changing the login window and it will reload the QSLs for the appropriate call sign.

The "End" date box is used when your first download times out due to a large number of QSLs (in the 10's of thousands). You can bracket the time and download a year or two at a time and eventually catch up. Once caught up this box should never need to be used again.

At any time you can click the Refresh button on the main window which will bring in new QSLs since the last time you checked. The Since date will automatically update and no other action is required except to click the Refresh button. You should never have to change the data in the boxes unless something goes wrong. If you ever get a new computer you just do the same steps again and you'll be right back where you were since the data in the program is synced to LOTW and does not depend on any local storage.

Map Display

The Map button will bring up a US map where you may select the band and mode to view. The mode pulldown only shows the modes you have actually worked on each band plus ALL and DIGITAL modes according to your LOTW account and notates any WAS completions by mode in green. The map can be resized, zoomed, and panned. Your map settings will be remembered across restarts.

If the Map is visible when you click Refresh you need to click the Map Redraw to update the map. This way you can see the visual change if you happen to get a QSL on the band selected when you Refresh your LOTW entries. I thought about making the map redraw automatic but I prefer to see the refresh independently.

The Lat/Lon boxes on the Map window are for your latitude/longitude coordinates in degrees (e.g. 31.909191, -91.343504 is my location in Quincy IL). Once set the Azimuth will indicate the azimuth to the mouse pointer from your QTH. You can click on the "Azimuth" label to toggle the azimuth data on/off if you don't want to see it.

The Label box will turn on state labels.

Grid Display

The grid display shows a WAS matrix of all bands for the selected mode and also ALL and DIGITAL modes. Pressing the "x" key will toggle the display from vertical to horizontal and back. If you hover over the state name you will see your top 10 QSO partners for that state (can be handy for a sked). You can hide a band or a state by double-clicking the header box at which point an "Unhide" button will appear so you can reset them all to be visible again.

The # of QSLs shown should match the # you see on https://lotw.arrl.org/lotwuser/qsostats - it is possible for this to get out of sync when changes are made to LOTW entries. You can reset the system by putting "1900-01-01" in the Since box and this will reload your data and the #'s should then match again.
